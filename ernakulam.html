Ernakulam

Ernakulam is a town as well as district was formed on April 1, 1958. The district includes Aluva, Kunnathunadu, Kochi, Kanayannur, Paravoor Taluks which was with Thrissur district earlier. Ernakulam is one of the most developed districts in Kerala. It was formed by the two princely states of Travancore and Cochin and the British Malabar. Being commercial hub of Kerala, all the major developments in any sector, it all happens here. Ernakulam is blessed with a wonderful culture and grandeur. It is the fact that Ernakulam is the first district in the whole country to have 100% literacy is a direct indicator of the enviable educational standards of its inhabitants. On the west side of Ernakulam there is Arabian Sea. The border is connected with Thrissur, Idukki, Alappuzha, and Kottayam Districts. Kerala's second largest river PERIYAR - flows through all the Taluks of this district except Muvattupuzha.

Cherai Beach

Kerala is bestowed with several precious destinations nestled amidst mother nature. One such adored treasure being, the Cherai beach, which is spread across 10 KM and is one of the most admired beach by visitors in Kerala. Situated in the suburbs of Kochi, Cherai is gifted with the splendour of nature in contrasting hues.

Marine Drive

Due to the glorious views of the backwaters and the Kochi harbor, taking a walk around the beach seems quite blissful. You can take a tour of the beach at any time of the day. There are plenty of restaurants and cafes around the beach, where you can even enjoy a perfect meal or an evening cup of coffee. One of the best things you can do here is to go for a boat ride.

Hill palace

Hill palace is an archaeological museum and palace located in the tripunithura neighbourhood of kochi city in the indian state of kerala.it is the largest archeaological museum in the state and was the imperial administrative office and official residence of the cochin maharaja.
